# Week 2 Exercise

## Part 1 — Linear Algebra (NumPy)

Write code to solve the following:

1. Create vector `v = [3, 4]`. Compute its magnitude (`||v||`).
2. Create a `(3, 3)` matrix `A` with random values. Compute its inverse and transpose.
3. Create two vectors and compute the cosine similarity between them without using scikit-learn.
4. Multiply a `(2, 3)` matrix by a `(3, 4)` matrix. What are the dimensions of the result? Why?

## Part 2 — Probability

Write a NumPy simulation to answer:

1. If you flip a coin 100 times, how many "heads" do you expect? Repeat the experiment 1000 times and plot a histogram.
2. Generate 1000 samples from a normal distribution with mean 50 and std 10. Compute the proportion of samples between 40 and 60.
3. Bayes problem: if 2% of people have a certain disease, and the test is 95% accurate, and a person tests positive — what is the probability they actually have the disease?

## Part 3 — Statistics

Use the Titanic dataset from Week 1:

1. Run a t-test between the ages of survivors and non-survivors. Is the difference statistically significant?
2. Compute the correlation between `Fare` and `Survived`. What does the number mean?
3. Give an example of correlation ≠ causation from the same dataset.
